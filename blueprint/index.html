Part 01

Funnel Strategy

One thesis, three moves, and the honest constraint we design around.

The thesis: demo-first, signup-late, publish-gated

Voice AI has a property almost no other SaaS category has: the product can prove itself in 45 seconds over the visitor's own phone. A restaurant owner doesn't need a features grid to believe — she needs to hear an agent take a reservation, handle "actually, make it 7:30," and confirm by text. That experience does the job of every testimonial, logo wall, and explainer video combined.

So the funnel is built on three moves:

Move 1 — Prove
The Instant Test Call

Every entry page leads to one action: enter your number, your phone rings in seconds, you talk to a Bamboo agent tuned to your industry. No signup. No email. The demo IS the ad.

Move 2 — Possess
Build Before You Sign Up

The free builder opens anonymously. Industry, goal, voice, personality — four choices in, the visitor has a named agent with a voice they picked. Email is asked only to save it. People don't abandon things they've named.

Move 3 — Publish
The Paywall Is the Phone Number

Everything is free until the moment of real-world value: connecting a live phone number. "Your agent is ready. Give it a phone line" is the cleanest upgrade trigger in the category.

The honest constraint: zero social proof today

The current site has no customers on display, and the live-call widget shows fabricated stats ("47 outbound calls, 12 qualified leads"). For an SMB owner burned by software before, invented proof is worse than none.

Non-negotiableWe do not fabricate logos, testimonials, or usage numbers. Until real ones exist, trust is built from things that are verifiably true right now: the live demo (proof by experience), transparent pricing with a 14-day money-back guarantee, a real founder with a name and a face, published latency/uptime numbers, and a security page written like adults wrote it. Every testimonial slot in this blueprint is marked [REAL-CUSTOMER] and ships hidden until filled.

Part 02

User Psychology

Who's on the other end of the click, what they're afraid of, and which levers actually move them.

The buyer

A local service business owner or office manager — dental practice, restaurant, real-estate team, gym, small law office. They answer (or fail to answer) their own phones. They are not evaluating "AI platforms"; they are trying to stop bleeding: missed calls at lunch rush, voicemail nobody checks, a receptionist who costs $3,200/month and quits in the spring.

Awareness stages → what each needs

StageThey're thinkingWe give them
Problem-aware"We keep missing calls and I know it's costing us."ROI calculator, "cost of a missed call" content, quiz
Solution-aware"Answering services exist… AI ones now too?"Comparison pages (vs. answering service, vs. voicemail, vs. hiring), the test call
Product-aware"Is Bamboo the one? Is it hard? What's the catch?"Builder, transparent pricing, guarantee, security page
Ready"Fine — but will it work on MY phones with MY hours?"Onboarding that imports their business in one field (website URL), publish flow with number porting help

The five objections, ranked, and their kill-shots

  1. "It'll embarrass me in front of customers." → The test call. Then: barge-in handling, human handoff shown in the demo script on purpose (the agent gracefully transfers). We show the failure mode being handled, not perfection.
  2. "This is going to be complicated." → "Built by 6,000 non-technical owners" only when true; until then: the 5-minute builder with a visible progress bar and a timer badge in marketing ("avg. setup: 4m 51s" — measured, real).
  3. "Hidden costs / minute-billing games." → Whole-number pricing, overage rate printed on the pricing card, "no contracts, cancel in two clicks" line.
  4. "What happens to my callers' data?" → Security page: recordings retention control, no training on your calls, HIPAA-readiness path for medical/dental (BAA on Growth+ when available — do not claim before true).
  5. "AI is a fad / I'll wait." → Loss framing: the calculator shows their monthly leak in dollars. Waiting has a price and we name it.

Emotional triggers we use (and two we refuse)

  • Relief — the dominant note. Copy voice: "the phone is handled." Not excitement — exhale.
  • Pride of authorship — the builder makes them a creator ("meet the employee you just built"). Named agent, chosen voice → endowment effect.
  • Loss aversion — missed-call math, always concrete and theirs (their volume × their ticket), never generic scare stats.
  • Status — "answers like a Fortune-500 front desk" for a taco shop. Small business, big-company phone presence.
  • We refuse: fake scarcity (countdown timers on evergreen pages) and fake social proof. Both poison SMB trust permanently.

Why visitors leave (friction inventory of the current site)

  • Nav promises five pages that are all the same page → broken-site smell. Fix: real pages or honest single-page anchors.
  • Zero proof + invented dashboard stats → skepticism at the exact moment of interest.
  • "Create Account" before any value → asks for commitment before demonstration.
  • $119.99 with no guarantee, no overage terms, no cancel terms → pricing anxiety.
  • No industry scent: an ad about dental offices lands on a generic page → message mismatch, instant bounce.

Part 04

Funnel Map

The full journey with every decision point and every recovery loop. Drop-offs aren't failures — each has a named catcher.

1
Ad / search result / organic link

Creative promise = page promise, always. Dental ad → dental page → dental-tuned agent.

2
Landing page (industry or home)

Decision: take the test call, start building, or bounce.

Bounce catcher → exit-intent: "Leave with the demo line — text yourself the number." (SMS capture, one field)
3
Test call — the proof moment

Phone rings in <10s. 45–90s scripted-but-live conversation. Ends with the agent saying: "Want one of me for your business? It takes about five minutes at mybamboo.ai/build."

No-answer catcher → SMS: "Missed us? Call this number anytime to talk to the demo agent: (xxx) xxx-xxxx"
4
Builder, steps 1–5 (anonymous)

Industry → goal → voice → personality → knowledge (paste website URL; we scrape hours/services/FAQs). Agent preview talks in-browser after step 3.

Abandon catcher → progress auto-saved in localStorage; return visit resumes with "Riley is 60% ready."
5
Email gate — "Save your agent"

Asked AFTER the agent exists and has a name. One field + magic link. No password.

Refusal catcher → "Keep building without saving" stays available; gate re-offers at publish.
6
Activation — test call on their own number

The aha: their agent answers THEIR questions about THEIR business on THEIR phone. Readiness score hits 100. Confetti (once).

Stall catcher → email D+1: "Riley answered 3 practice questions perfectly. Hear the recording."
7
Publish gate — the paywall

"Give Riley a phone line." Plan picker inline; card; 14-day guarantee restated at the button.

Hesitation catcher → 7-day email sequence (Part 09 of copy), each email a use case + one CTA. Retargeting audience `activated_not_paid`.
8
Paid → expansion

Live agent → weekly "calls handled" digest email → usage-based nudges ("Riley hit 80% of minutes") → Growth upsell → referral loop ("give a month, get a month") → multi-location / white-label → Scale conversation.

9
Optional human track (any point)

"Prefer a walkthrough?" persistent quiet link → /demo calendar. Never modal, never pushy — this is the escape valve for the 15% who want a human.

FAQ (homepage six)

Will callers know it's AI? Your agent introduces itself honestly and sounds natural. Most callers just care that someone answered and helped. You choose the voice and how it identifies itself.

What if it can't answer something? It transfers to you (or takes a message with a callback promise) — your choice, per situation. You'll hear the whole exchange in the transcript.

Do I need to change my phone number? No. Forward your existing line, or get a new number from us. Both take minutes.

How long does setup really take? Under five minutes with a website. About ten if you type your details by hand. You can test before anyone else ever hears it.

What happens if I go over my minutes? Calls keep getting answered at $0.35/minute — we never send your callers to voicemail because of a plan limit. You'll get a heads-up at 80%.

Can I cancel? Two clicks, from your dashboard, any time. First 14 days: full refund, no questions.

Part 08

Design System

"Field & Line" (v2) — a daylight field with night sections, threaded by the phone line. Leaf-white ground, green-black ink, deep bamboo green, and an amber voicemail-lamp accent for the missed-call world. Monospace stays reserved for what machines say: transcripts, numbers, phone digits.

Color

Field #F5F7F0page ground (green-biased, not cream)
Ink #111C15text — a green-black, not neutral black
Bamboo #1B7449primary actions · brand
Sprout #3FA972accent on night grounds
Night #0B1712after-hours sections: before/after, pricing, final CTA
Lamp #DE9F35the voicemail light — missed-call states, LIVE indicator
  • Lamp amber is semantic, not decorative: it marks the world without Bamboo (blinking voicemail, missed calls) and the live-call indicator. It never appears on buttons.
  • Night sections are deep green-black (not neutral dark) with Sprout green — the page alternates field/night for rhythm, and night is where the product's promise lives (the call answered after dark).
  • Contrast: all text ≥ 4.5:1; green-on-field buttons use white text on #1B7449.

Both webfonts ship inlined as woff2 data URIs (latin subsets, ~165 KB total): zero extra requests, zero FOUT, no CDN dependency — LCP stays a conversion feature. The mono-for-machine convention carries over from v1 and remains the brand's signature. Structural motifs: the node (a small ring marking every section eyebrow and each step on the line) and the culm (the builder's readiness meter — eight internodes, one grown per step, leaves at real milestones).

Space, radius, elevation

  • Spacing scale: 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96 / 128. Sections breathe at 96–128 desktop, 64 mobile.
  • Grid: 12-col, 1120px max content, 24px gutters.
  • Radius: inputs/buttons 10px, cards 14px, panes 20px. One family, no pill buttons except tags.
  • Shadow: two levels only — resting (0 1px 2px rgba(16,21,18,.06)) and lifted (0 12px 32px −12px rgba(16,21,18,.18)). Elevation means interactivity.

Motion

  • Signature moment: the ring pulse — concentric rings emanating from the PhoneCTA while your phone is being called. It's the brand animation; nothing else on the page moves while it runs.
  • Builder signature: the culm growth — each completed step grows one internode of the readiness stalk (400ms fill); leaves unfold at voice, knowledge, and test milestones. The chrome progress bar is the same culm lying down: eight segments, not a continuous fill.
  • The lamp blink (2s opacity cycle) is reserved for exactly two places: the live-call indicator and the before-Bamboo voicemail light. If everything blinks, nothing is live.
  • Scroll reveals: opacity 0→1 + 12px rise, 450ms cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.3,1), stagger 60ms, once only.
  • Transcript typing: 24 chars/sec with 300ms breath between speakers — fast enough to feel live, slow enough to read.
  • Count-ups on ROI numbers: 900ms ease-out. Confetti: exactly once, at readiness 100%, 1.8s, then never again for that user.
  • prefers-reduced-motion: all of the above become static end-states. The site must fully work with zero animation.

Accessibility baseline

  • Every interactive element keyboard-reachable with a visible 2px Bamboo focus ring.
  • Audio components ship transcripts; the demo call has a text-chat equivalent ("Talk in browser" has a type-instead mode).
  • Form errors: text + icon + described-by, never color alone. Touch targets ≥ 44px.

Part 11

Developer Notes

How to build this so it's fast, testable, and doesn't rot.

  • Framework: Next.js (or Astro) with static generation for all marketing pages; industry pages are one template + a content collection (JSON per vertical: agent name, H1, pains, integrations, FAQ). Adding vertical #9 is a content PR, not a build.
  • Performance budget (paid pages): LCP < 1.8s on 4G, CLS < 0.05, JS < 120KB gzipped before the PhoneCTA hydrates. Transcript animation is CSS + a 3KB script, not a motion library. No third-party tag managers on landing pages — analytics loads after first interaction or 3s idle.
  • PhoneCTA backend: rate-limit by IP + number hash (3 calls/number/day), country allowlist, verify via the call itself (answering = verification). Store consent timestamp for the post-demo SMS. Fallback chain: phone-out fails → offer browser call → offer callable demo line.
  • Builder state: localStorage until email; server-side after. Resume link in save email deep-links to exact step. All copy strings in one file — copy tests without deploys (PostHog feature flags hold variants).
  • Website scrape (step 5): timeout at 12s with graceful partial results ("Got your hours and services — add prices manually?"). Never block the flow on the crawler.
  • Checkout: Stripe Checkout (hosted) at launch — it's the trust-maximal, PCI-free path. Inline Elements later if data justifies.
  • Event dispatch: one track(event, props) util fanning out to GA4/PostHog/CAPI. Nobody calls a vendor SDK directly. Server-side events (call completed, payment) go straight from backend to CAPI/GA4 Measurement Protocol — the browser is not the source of truth for money events.
  • Testimonial discipline in code: TestimonialBand takes a required verified: true prop sourced from a content file with name/business/date; renders nothing otherwise. Make fake proof structurally hard.
  • SEO plumbing: FAQPage + Product schema, per-industry title/meta from the content collection, comparisons and /learn statically rendered, sitemap auto-generated.
Part 12

Mobile Experience

70%+ of paid-social traffic is a phone held in one hand — and the product is literally about phones. Mobile is the primary design target, not the adaptation.

  • The demo is one thumb-tap better on mobile: the PhoneCTA becomes "📞 Call me" with the number field using type="tel" + autofill; on iOS/Android we also offer "Call the demo line" (tap-to-dial a real number) — zero typing, the single lowest-friction demo on the site.
  • Hero stacks: headline → phone input → transcript pane (auto-playing, 60% viewport height max). Proof strip becomes a 2×2 grid.
  • StickyMobileCTA after 60% scroll; disappears when any input is focused (keyboard collision) and on pricing/checkout.
  • Builder: one question per screen already — on mobile each option is a full-width 56px row; voice previews auto-stop on scroll; progress is a thin top bar + % only.
  • Tables (pricing matrix, comparisons) collapse to stacked cards with a "compare" toggle that scrolls horizontally in its own container.
  • Tap targets 44px minimum; the FAQ accordion rows are 56px. No hover-dependent information anywhere.
Part 13

Desktop Experience

Search and evaluation traffic skews desktop — the comparison shopper, the office manager with two tabs of competitors open. Desktop's job is confidence at a glance.

  • Hero split: left column = words + PhoneCTA; right column = TranscriptPane playing continuously. The transcript is the "product screenshot" this product doesn't otherwise have.
  • Persistent quiet nav CTA ("Try it free") that swaps to "Resume building — Riley is 60% ready" for returning builder-abandoners (localStorage).
  • Pricing page: 4 cards in one row, Growth elevated 8px with the lifted shadow; feature matrix below with a sticky header row.
  • Industry pages: two-column "What Mia handles" checklist next to the mini-ROI sliders — evaluation content side-by-side instead of stacked.
  • Subtle mouse-parallax (≤6px translate) on the hero transcript pane only. Nothing else follows the cursor.
  • Keyboard path: tab order lands on the phone input first; Enter starts the call. The whole funnel is completable without a mouse.
